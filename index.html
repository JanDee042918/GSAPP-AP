Based on a previous survey of the 2012 Masters of Architecture class, the GSAPP - ALUMNI PROJECT attempts to capture the state and evolution of GSAPP Alumni five years out. By comparing and contrasting the different disciplines and classes, it provides insight into GSAPP classes and helps record the paths GSAPP graduates have taken.

POST

Overall most of the students that come into GSAPP have some previous professional experience in their field of choice. However, there are some interesting things. For example, even though the AADs are on average younger than the M.Archs they seem to come with more professional experience than them. In addition, the bit outliers are the MS.RED students, which in general come into their program with much more professional experience than the students for the rest of the school. The opposite would be the HP students, most of which have less than 1 year of professional experience.

Salaries... some majors (certain years) are difficult to judge because there aren't that many people. However, for others, and the overall trends, this graphic is pretty telling. For example, the RED students have a much much higher percentage of people in the top bracket. The HP students would be the opposite. Also, we see a penalty on the AADs and AUD students. Supposing most of them are foreigners and don't have the connections that the M.Arch have, their salaries are lower. Also, although it is difficult to judge 2009, you can say that one surprising thing is that the class of 2012 is doing marginally better than the class of 2010, even though they have one year less of eperience.

Salary By Year / Major

Now, are the salaries related to the type of software the alumni are using? One would expect that to be the case. Specially for the people using very "advanced" software (ie. Grasshopper, 3dsMax, or the scripting languages). However, looking at the data this intuition doesn't prove true. On the contrary, the more you climb in the salaries ladder, the more common the software. People that are making more than $80,000 tend to use mostly Microsoft Office (specially Excel and Word). On the other extreme are the people making less than $30,000, using mostly Photoshop. Overall, however, the prevalance of Office is astonishing. One would think that after going to gradschool at GSAPP, with all it's technological sophistication, more people would be using cutting edge software. But on the contrary, the software picture seems almost banal.
